Born Out of Frustration. Driven by Culture.

I started AfriShip because I saw something broken — and I couldn't ignore it. For years, I watched friends and family in the diaspora struggle to stay connected to home. Birthdays passed without hot Amala and Efo Riro. Holidays came and went without dry fish, kuli-kuli, or Ankara wrappers.

And when they did try to bring Nigeria closer — the cost was outrageous, the packaging untrustworthy, or the goods arrived late, spoiled, or confiscated at customs.The global carriers didn't get it. The culture, the care, the nuances — they were lost in translation.

The global carriers didn't get it. The culture, the care, the nuances — they were lost in translation. That’s when I knew: If nobody would build a shipping solution with Africa at heart, I would.AfriShip was born out of frustration — and fueled by love.

Love for our people. Love for our culture. Love for what connects us, even when we’re thousands of miles apart.

We are not just shipping goods —

We are delivering memories.

We are preserving traditions.

We are connecting families across continents.

From the first dried fish we carefully packaged to boxes we now prepare monthly, every shipment is treated with the kind of care your mama would give it.

We partner with trusted transport and airport logistics teams, and we handle sourcing, packaging, labeling, and compliance ourselves — because excellence is our export.

AfriShip is designed to be simple, reliable, and fast.

No more fear of delays. No more missing items. No more insane pricing.

Whether it’s garri Ijebu, ofada rice, dried snails, or aso-oke, we ensure it gets there — beautifully, safely, and on time.

Today, we serve Nigerians in Europe and North America, and we’re expanding to meet demand from across the globe. Our goal is clear:

To become the most trusted export brand for African products worldwide.

But it’s more than business.
